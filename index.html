What Is Phishing

Phishing is a hacking attempt where a hacker tries to obtain secure information in order to gain access to an individuals account. The most common type of phishing attempt is sent via email; however a phishing attempt can be sent through other channels as well.

The best way to full understand how a phishing attack can happen is to give you an example scenario...

You Receive an Email

It's a simple password reset email, like this:

You've seen plenty of emails like this before and the email looks like it's coming from a trustworthy source. You want to make sure that nothing strange is happening to your account so you decide to follow the instructions.

It seems like your friends at Netflix have your back, so you click on the 'Reset Password' button.

You Reset Your Password

looks like the typical password reset page:

Everything seems pretty straight forward, in front of you is your typical Netflix reset password page.

In order to prevent someone from gaining access to your account you reluctantly reset your password, click save, and then...

And Then

You just got Phished!

Were you able to catch it?

In the previous step you didn't send your password to Netflix, you sent it to another website, take a look at that URL again... There is an extra"x"in the domain.

Hackers use this trick and many others to obtain your info.

What really happened behind the scenes?

The hacker cloned a Netflix page and sent you aphishing email.
You enter your password, click 'save', and the hacker now has your (unencrypted) password.
Now, they can access this account and possibly many others.

How can you prevent this?

There are many ways to prevent a phishing or hacking attempt from occurring, here are just a few tips

  • Double check the sender address of an email
  • Double check domains before entering sensitive info
  • Don't use the same password for every account
  • Enable Two-Factor authentication when possible
  • Learn more about security awareness with Curricula

If you believe you are in the presence of a phishing or hacking attempt be sure to double check the identity of the attempt and notify your IT Department.
